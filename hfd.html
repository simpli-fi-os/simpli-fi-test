The Mechanics

We Don't Just Hold. We Harvest.

Traditional portfolios rely on one engine: asset appreciation. We add a second: systematic volatility harvesting.

Step 1

Sell Volatility

We sell cash-secured puts on high-quality ETFs at strikes 7–10% below market. This generates immediate cash premiums while waiting for a better entry price. If the market stays flat or rises, we keep the premium and repeat.

Step 2

Acquire & Enhance

If assigned, we acquire the asset at a structural discount. We then sell covered calls to generate a second income stream. Dividends from quality holdings like SCHD and TLT add a third source of yield.

Step 3

Compound Tax-Free

In a DAF or IRA, all three income streams — option premium, dividends, and capital gains — compound without tax drag. This is the structural advantage that makes the strategy work.

Ideal Client Profile

Designed for Tax-Advantaged Compounding.

This strategy generates significant short-term capital gains via option premiums. It is structurally optimized for accounts where those gains compound tax-free.

  • Donor Advised Funds: Tax-free growth for philanthropy. Premium income and dividends compound without drag.
  • Foundations & Endowments: Consistent income for grant-making with a yield buffer that softens spending-rate risk.
  • IRA / Roth IRA: Tax-sheltered accumulation. The concave payoff is offset by decades of compounding.
  • Family & Legacy Accounts: Multi-generational wealth building with institutional discipline and full liquidity.
Taxable Account Warning: In standard brokerage accounts, option premiums are generally treated as short-term capital gains (ordinary income rates), which may significantly reduce after-tax returns. Consult your tax advisor before implementing in a taxable account.

Risk Disclosure

This Is Not a Bond Replacement. This strategy involves equity risk. Selling puts creates an obligation to buy shares during market declines. When the market drops sharply, the portfolio will draw down.

Concave Payoff Profile. Options strategies produce frequent small gains and infrequent larger losses. Standard risk metrics like Sharpe ratio may overstate risk-adjusted performance relative to strategies with symmetric payoffs.

Drawdown Risk. While premiums and dividends provide a buffer, significant market crashes (>20% drops) will result in real portfolio losses. This is not a principal-protected product. There is no structural floor.

Correlation Regime Risk. Historical correlations between asset classes (e.g., stocks vs. bonds) may not persist. During the 2022 inflation shock, both equities and long-duration bonds declined simultaneously.
